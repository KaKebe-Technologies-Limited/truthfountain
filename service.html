Active Volunteers

Children of the Nations Uganda (COTN)

Founded in 2006, this ministry was a response to the 3 decades of rebel mayhem in Northern Uganda that resulted in countless orphans and destitute children left without shelter, food, healthcare, and education. To date, 723 children are directly supported at this program.

COTN Uganda campus has been providing vital care and services to these children in need.

Saving Grace Nursery and Primary School

Prior to this program, street kids in Lira had never had a program dedicated to serving their needs. In 2009, a partnership trust was created that began this program, now helping about 200 children access free shelter, medicine, and education.

Saving Grace Uganda continues to provide support to these children, offering shelter, healthcare, and education to ensure their future success.

Truth Vocational and Technical Institute

In 2013, we started a skills training institute that would assist students who had dropped out of secondary (high) school for lack of school fees and other causes. Here, we offer courses such as Block Laying (building), Carpentry, Tailoring and Garment Cutting, Mechanics and Driving, Beauty and Hairdressing, and Hotel Management/Catering.

My Sister’s Keeper Project

My Sister’s Keeper is a counseling and skills training program aimed at reducing sexual exploitation and poverty on the streets of Lira by educating, empowering, and supporting former sex workers in healing and pursuing new careers. This project helps these women build sustainable businesses and livelihoods to become self-reliant after the rehabilitation process.

The project, located within Lira town, works closely with the local community, church influencers, and various partners to rehabilitate and empower the victims of sexual exploitation and former commercial sex workers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to your most common questions about our programs and initiatives.

What is the Patriotize event, and how does it impact the youth in Lango Sub-Region?

The Patriotize event is a youth empowerment program focused on strengthening communities through leadership, economic stability, and sustainable agricultural practices. It aims to help young people take charge of tomorrow by learning valuable skills today.

How does ICT play a role in promoting security and prosperity in the region?

ICT is a vital enabler in driving security and prosperity. By integrating technology, we can enhance economic stability, foster efficient agricultural practices, and provide young people with tools to succeed in their careers and communities.

What is the role of commercial agriculture in strengthening the Lango Sub-Region economy?

Commercial agriculture, especially in sectors like fishing and crop farming, plays a key role in boosting the local economy. With value addition and industrialization, it can create jobs and enhance trade both locally and internationally.

How are the grants for student projects contributing to community development?

Through mentorship and financial support, these grants encourage students to develop impactful projects that address local challenges, such as climate action, agricultural security, and economic empowerment.

What is the importance of green skills and sustainable practices in youth development?

Green skills are essential for building sustainable careers and addressing environmental challenges. They empower young people to actively participate in climate action and contribute to sustainable development in Uganda and beyond.

What partnerships are crucial for the success of the Student Climate Action Summit?

Key partnerships with organizations like UNDP, the National Youth Council, NEMA, and various academic institutions are essential for the success of the Student Climate Action Summit. These partnerships enable collaboration and bring together experts who can guide youth in impactful climate action.
